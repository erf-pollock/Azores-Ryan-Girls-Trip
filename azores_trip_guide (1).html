🏡 Our houses
🏠 Casa do Mar ↗🏠 Casa da Ilha ↗
São Miguel is known as the "Green Island" — a wild, living landscape of crater lakes, volcanic hot springs, dramatic cliffs, whale-filled Atlantic waters, and endless hydrangea-lined roads. It manages to be simultaneously adventurous and deeply relaxing. There is something here for every single person in this group.
Our houses are in Ponta Garça, on the south-central coast — a perfect home base. Ponta Delgada (the main city) is 20 min west. Furnas valley is 40 min east. The Vila Franca islet is 5 minutes from our door.
Orientation
East vs. West

The island splits naturally into two sides. We've planned dedicated days for each so no one is doubling back.

⬅ West Side

40–50 min from house
  • Sete Cidades crater lakes (the iconic postcard view)
  • Vista do Rei viewpoint
  • Boca do Inferno viewpoint (short hike)
  • Caldeira Velha hot springs (jungle waterfall)
  • Ponta da Ferraria ocean hot spring
  • Mosteiros coastline

➡ East Side

20–60 min from house
  • Furnas valley — hot springs + volcanic stew
  • Terra Nostra Garden + thermal pool
  • Nordeste — dramatic cliffs, quiet viewpoints
  • Ponta do Sossego clifftop garden
  • Lagoa do Congro (hidden crater lake)
  • Gorreana Tea Plantation (north coast)
Action items
Before we leave
🐋
Book whale watching 4–6 weeks out
Futurismo Azores Adventures and Terra Azul both depart from near our house in Vila Franca do Campo. June is peak season for sperm whales, blue whales, and dolphins. Groups of 12 need early booking. Confirm they can take a group our size.
🍲
Reserve Cozido das Furnas for Furnas day
This legendary volcanic stew is cooked to order underground and restaurants can run out. Book Tony's Restaurante or Caldeiras & Vulcões in Furnas well in advance. Mention your party size and dietary restrictions. Vegetarian options available at some spots.
🍽️
Make dinner reservations for group dinners out
A group of 12 requires advance booking everywhere. Priority dinners: final night at Tasquinha Vieira or Alcides (Ponta Delgada), and the Sunday dinner after hiking. A Tasca does NOT take reservations — arrive before 6pm.
🏝️
Vila Franca Islet: boat tour only (not swimming)
The islet only opens for swimming on June 15 — our trip ends June 6. But guided boat tours around the volcanic crater run year-round and are genuinely spectacular. Book through Terra Azul or Azores Whale Watch. About 1 hour, biologist-guided.
🌫️
Download an Azores webcam app
São Miguel weather changes fast. Sete Cidades can go from clear to completely fogged over in minutes — and there's nothing to see if that happens. Check the live webcams every morning before driving to viewpoints. Plan B days are built into the itinerary.
👙
Pack dark/old swimsuits for hot springs
The iron-rich thermal water at Furnas (Terra Nostra and Poça da Dona Beija) permanently stains light-colored swimwear orange. This is not a warning to be ignored. Pack old swimsuits or specifically dark ones for hot spring days.
💶
Bring cash in euros
Small villages, trail parking, and some local restaurants are cash-only. ATMs are available in Ponta Delgada and Ribeira Grande. Stock up when you arrive. Cards are accepted in most larger restaurants and shops in the city.

Day 1 · Saturday, May 30
Arrival + Ponta Delgada
Easy arrival day
Morning

Land at João Paulo II Airport (PDL). Pick up the two vans from the rental counter. 10 minutes from the airport into the city center.

First stop: coffee and pastéis de nata at a café in Ponta Delgada. Welcome to Portugal!

Afternoon

Explore Ponta Delgada on foot: walk through the Portas da Cidade (the iconic triple arch), browse the waterfront promenade, peek into the Mercado da Graça. Get a feel for the city.

Pineapple plantation visit — Augusto Arruda plantation is just outside Ponta Delgada. A fascinating, tucked-away greenhouse operation. Perfect for a first-day activity: low energy, fascinating, and delicious tastings.

Grocery run at Continente or Pingo Doce for house staples.

💡 The Azorean greenhouse pineapple is unlike any pineapple you've had. Each one takes 2 years to grow. Buy a few to bring back to the houses.
Evening

Drive to houses in Ponta Garça (~20 min from Ponta Delgada). Check in, unpack, take in the view. Dinner at the house — keep it simple. Early night; it's been a travel day.

Easy day No bookings needed Perfect for jet lag
Day 2 · Sunday, May 31
Gorreana Tea + Local Hike
Gentle start to exploring
Morning

Breakfast at the house. Drive north (~40 min) to Gorreana Tea Plantation — Europe's only tea plantation, family-owned since 1883. Walk the rolling green rows, visit the old factory, taste the teas for free. Genuinely beautiful and completely relaxed.

💡 Go any time — it's always open and free. The teas are delicate, bright, and nothing like supermarket tea. Buy some to bring home.
Afternoon

Lunch at a café near Porto Formoso on the north coast (Cantinho do Cais is a local favorite). Then the group splits by energy level:

Adventurous group: Salto do Cabrito Waterfall trail (medium, ~5 miles) or Rocha da Relva Coastal Trail.

Relaxed group: Pinhal da Paz Forest Reserve in Ponta Delgada — a flat, shaded 2-mile forest walk. Genuinely lovely and completely manageable.

💡 Both groups end up back in Ponta Delgada area — easy to reconvene for dinner.
Evening

Dinner out together in Ponta Delgada — first group dinner of the trip! Try A Tasca (lively, Azorean classics, no reservations — arrive before 6pm) or book ahead at another restaurant. Good wines, fresh seafood, and the full São Miguel experience.

Split-group friendly Book dinner ahead Arrive at A Tasca before 6pm
Day 3 · Monday, June 1
Sete Cidades — the Iconic West
The flagship day
Very Early

Leave by 7:30am. This is important — Vista do Rei parking fills by 9am, and fog can roll in by mid-morning and ruin everything. Check the webcam before leaving.

⚠️ Sete Cidades fog warning: if the webcam shows thick cloud, consider flipping this day with another. The lakes are invisible from above in heavy fog — there's nothing to see.
Morning

Miradouro Vista do Rei — the postcard view of the Azores. Twin blue and green volcanic crater lakes spread out below you. This is one of the most breathtaking views in the Atlantic. Walk the old Monte Palace hotel ruins for an even higher angle.

Boca do Inferno viewpoint — a short 1.2km walk from Lagoa do Canário parking. The other angle: panoramic views of both lakes plus the Atlantic beyond. Worth every step.

💡 The two viewpoints are about 10 min drive apart. Do Vista do Rei first (more accessible), then Boca do Inferno.
Midday

Drive down into the caldera to the tiny village of Sete Cidades. Lunch at one of the small local spots in the village. Then the group splits:

Adventurous group: Vista do Rei to Sete Cidades hike (7.5 mi, 1,459 ft elevation, ~5 hrs) or Serra Devassa loop (2–2.5 hrs, beautiful 360° views). Stunning, uncrowded, world-class.

Relaxed group: Stroll or rent bikes around the flat lake edge. Walk the old aqueduct. Explore the village. Sit by the water.

Afternoon

Caldeira Velha hot springs on the drive home (~€10/person). A hidden gem: a lush jungle waterfall with a geothermal pool underneath it. Completely different vibe from Furnas — smaller, wilder, incredibly atmospheric. Everyone can enjoy this.

⚠️ Dark swimsuits only — the water is iron-rich and stains.
Evening

Return home. Sunset from the house, dinner in or nearby in Vila Franca do Campo. Full day — everyone will sleep well.

Leave by 7:30am Dark swimsuits for springs Split-group friendly Check webcam first!
Day 4 · Tuesday, June 2
Furnas — Volcano Day
A full group day
Morning

Caldeiras das Furnas (free, open air): walk among active fumaroles, boiling mud pots, and sulphur steam vents at Lagoa das Furnas. Around noon, watch staff pull out the cozido pots that have been cooking underground in the volcanic soil since morning. This is unlike anything you've ever seen.

Miradouro do Pico do Ferro for a beautiful high view over the whole valley.

💡 The sulphur smell hits you as you arrive in Furnas — locals call it "the Furnas smell." It fades after a few minutes. Lean into it.
Midday

Cozido das Furnas — pre-booked at Tony's Restaurante or Caldeiras & Vulcões. A meat and vegetable stew slow-cooked underground for 5+ hours by volcanic heat alone. Pork, beef, chicken, chouriço, cabbage, potatoes, carrots. One of the most unique meals on earth.

⚠️ Must be reserved in advance — restaurants prepare it to order and quantities are limited.
Afternoon

Hot springs — choose your vibe:

Terra Nostra Park (€16/person): world-famous botanical garden + a huge iron-orange thermal pool (38–42°C / 100–108°F). Renovated in 2024. Incredible garden to explore before soaking. Go here for the full experience.

Poça da Dona Beija: 5 pools at different temperatures, shallower and more casual, open until 10pm. Better for those who want a lighter experience or prefer a cooler pool.

⚠️ Dark swimsuits. Both pools have iron-rich water that stains permanently.
Evening

Optional detour: Bar da Caloura (30 min from Furnas on the way home) for fresh seafood right on the water at sunset. One of the best local restaurants on the island — write your name on the slate and wait. Or head home for a relaxed house dinner.

Pre-book cozido — essential Dark swimsuits! Everyone together Terra Nostra €16/person
Day 5 · Wednesday, June 3
Whale Watching + Free Afternoon
Most memorable day
Morning

Whale watching — departs from Vila Franca do Campo marina (5 min from our house) or Ponta Delgada. ~3 hours on the water. The Azores is one of the world's top whale watching destinations with 20+ species of cetaceans. June is excellent for sperm whales, common and bottlenose dolphins, and sometimes blue whales.

Recommended operators: Futurismo Azores Adventures or Terra Azul. Both use land-based spotters with binoculars to locate animals before the boats head out — very high success rate.

⚠️ Book 4–6 weeks in advance for a group of 12. Confirm your operator can accommodate everyone together.
Afternoon

Return, dry off, recover. Completely free afternoon — pool at the house, explore Vila Franca do Campo village, or take a short walk up to the Chapel of Nossa Senhora da Paz for stunning tiled staircases and panoramic views over town.

💡 The chapel staircase is one of those hidden gems that isn't in any guidebook. 10-minute walk from the village center.
Evening

Two options — the group can split or pick one together:

Sunset catamaran cruise — Futurismo South Coast Catamaran or VIP Sunset Sailing (Captain Luis, Ponta Delgada). Beautiful way to see the island from the water at golden hour.

Nice dinner out — a slightly more special restaurant night in Ponta Delgada. Alcides for steak, or book a group table at O Calheta for modern Azorean sharing plates.

Pre-book whale watching Everyone in the water Sunset cruise or dinner
Day 6 · Thursday, June 4
Nordeste — The Quiet East
Hidden gem day
Morning

Drive east (~1 hr) to Nordeste — the far eastern corner of São Miguel and the least-visited, most dramatic part of the island. Spectacular cliffs, wildflowers, lush green valleys, and almost no tourists.

Ponta do Sossego: a manicured clifftop garden perched over the Atlantic. Free, peaceful, and genuinely beautiful. Benches everywhere — perfect for the aunts to sit and take it all in.

Ponta da Madrugada: another dramatic clifftop viewpoint. Less visited and incredibly scenic.

Midday

Lunch in Nordeste village — small, authentic, local. The real Azores.

On the drive back west, detour to Lagoa do Congro — an off-the-beaten-path crater lake accessible via a short forest hike (about 30 min round trip). Fluorescent green water, absolute silence, birds, ferns. One of the most magical spots on the island and almost no one goes.

💡 Look for the Lagoa do Congro exit off the main EN1-1A about 10 min past Furnas heading west. Easy to miss — look it up in Maps before you go.
Afternoon

Back home by 3–4pm. This is a great house day option for anyone who wants to skip the drive and just relax, swim, read, and cook. The adventurous group does Nordeste; the relaxed crew stays at the pool. No pressure either way.

Evening

Dinner at home — cook together. At this point in the trip you'll want a cozy night in.

Optional house day for relaxed crew Hidden gem: Lagoa do Congro Far east of island
Day 7 · Friday, June 5
Vila Franca Islet + Last Evening
Last full day — make it count
Morning

Vila Franca do Campo Islet boat tour — right outside our door. A guided zodiac or e-boat tour around the "Princess Ring," a perfectly circular submerged volcanic crater rising from the sea about 500 meters offshore. About 1 hour, guided by a biologist or naturalist.

Note: the islet's interior lagoon only opens for swimming on June 15 this year, so we'll do the boat tour around it rather than swimming inside — still spectacular.

💡 Book through Terra Azul or Azores Whale Watch. The biologist commentary on the geology and marine life is genuinely fascinating.
Afternoon

Return to Ponta Delgada area. Visit a pineapple plantation if you didn't on arrival day — Augusto Arruda or Pineapple Plantation dos Açores. Greenhouse tours, tastings, and the chance to buy Azorean pineapple to bring home.

Last afternoon at the houses: swim, pack, unwind. Soak up the last of it.

Evening

Final dinner out — the last night together deserves something special. Book ahead in Ponta Delgada:

Tasquinha Vieira: the best restaurant on the island. Modern Azorean fusion, seasonal sharing plates, exceptional wine list. The move for a celebratory group dinner — reserve well in advance for 12.

Alcides: legendary Azorean steak with pimenta salgada (salty pickled peppers). More traditional, equally wonderful — a fitting last meal on the island.

💡 After dinner, take a slow walk along the Ponta Delgada waterfront promenade. Harbor lights, warm June air, last night in the Azores. 🌊

Insider knowledge
Tips & tricks
🌫️

Check webcams every morning

São Miguel weather changes fast. Sete Cidades viewpoints can be completely fogged over. Check live webcam apps before driving 45 min to find nothing. We've built in flexibility for exactly this.

⏰

Early bird wins at viewpoints

Vista do Rei parking fills by 9–10am on clear days. Arrive before 9am for the best light, fewest people, and a parking spot. Worth the early alarm.

👙

Dark swimsuits for hot springs

Iron-rich water at Furnas's hot springs permanently stains light-colored swimwear orange. This is a serious warning. Pack dark or old swimsuits specifically for hot spring days.

🌧️

Always pack a rain layer

Even on beautiful days, mountain temperatures drop and showers appear out of nowhere. A packable rain jacket takes up no space and saves the day constantly. Layers are essential.

💶

Carry cash

Small villages, trail parking, and some local restaurants only accept cash. Get euros at an ATM in Ponta Delgada when you arrive. Cards work fine in the city and most restaurants.

🚗

Driving is easy

Roads are well-maintained, GPS works perfectly, and traffic is light. Mountain roads can be narrow — take them slowly. Drive on the right. No toll roads on São Miguel.

🍽️

Restaurants open late

Most restaurants don't open for dinner until 6:30–7pm. Don't show up at 6pm expecting to eat. Plan afternoon snacks if hunger strikes earlier.

💰

Tipping culture

Tipping is not obligatory in Portugal, but rounding up to the nearest 10% is always appreciated for good service. Never expected, always welcomed.

📱

Your US phone works

Portugal is EU — most US plans include international data. Confirm with your carrier before leaving. Google Maps works well across the island for navigation.

    • 🔌
    Power adapters — everyone needs one
    Portugal (including the Azores) uses Type F plugs — two round pins, 230V / 50Hz. US plugs (flat two-prong or three-prong) will not fit. Pick up a Type F adapter or a universal travel adapter before you leave — they're cheap on Amazon and hard to find once you're there. The good news: all modern phones, laptops, and camera chargers are dual-voltage (100–240V), so you only need the plug adapter, not a separate voltage converter. Check your device's power brick — it will say "100-240V" if it's compatible. One adapter per person is ideal; a multi-port USB charging hub + one adapter is even better for shared rooms.

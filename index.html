🏡 Our houses
🏠 Casa do Mar ↗🏠 Casa da Ilha ↗
São Miguel is known as the "Green Island" — a wild, living landscape of crater lakes, volcanic hot springs, dramatic cliffs, whale-filled Atlantic waters, and endless hydrangea-lined roads. It manages to be simultaneously adventurous and deeply relaxing. There is something here for every single person in this group.
Our houses are in Ponta Garça, on the south-central coast — a perfect home base. Ponta Delgada (the main city) is 20 min west. Furnas valley is 40 min east. The Vila Franca islet is 5 minutes from our door.

Day 1 · Saturday, May 30
Arrival + Ponta Delgada
Easy arrival day
Morning

Land at João Paulo II Airport (PDL). Pick up the two vans from the rental counter. 10 minutes from the airport into the city center.

First stop: coffee and pastéis de nata at a café in Ponta Delgada. Welcome to Portugal!

Afternoon

Explore Ponta Delgada on foot: walk through the Portas da Cidade (the iconic triple arch), browse the waterfront promenade, peek into the Mercado da Graça. Get a feel for the city.

Pineapple plantation visit — Augusto Arruda plantation is just outside Ponta Delgada. A fascinating, tucked-away greenhouse operation. Perfect for a first-day activity: low energy, fascinating, and delicious tastings.

Grocery run at Continente or Pingo Doce for house staples.

💡 The Azorean greenhouse pineapple is unlike any pineapple you've had. Each one takes 2 years to grow. Buy a few to bring back to the houses.
Evening

Drive to houses in Ponta Garça (~20 min from Ponta Delgada). Check in, unpack, take in the view. Dinner at the house — keep it simple. Early night; it's been a travel day.

Easy day No bookings needed Perfect for jet lag
Day 2 · Sunday, May 31
Lagoa do Fogo + Ribeira Grande + Salto do Cabrito
The best single day on the island
Early AM

Leave by 7:30–8:00am. Drive 20 min west toward the center of the island. Check the webcam before leaving — Lagoa do Fogo sits in the clouds and can be socked in by 10am.

⚠️ Fog warning: same rule as Sete Cidades. If the crater is in cloud, flip the morning and start with Ribeira Grande instead, then return to Lagoa do Fogo mid-afternoon when it often clears.
Morning

Lagoa do Fogo — "Lake of Fire" — the hike you don't want to miss. A turquoise volcanic crater lake in the island's interior, surrounded by jungle and lava channels. The trail (PRC03) winds through dense endemic forest before the lagoon opens up in front of you in a way that genuinely stops you in your tracks. Arguably the most beautiful single view on São Miguel — and it's only 20 minutes from our house.

Trail stats: 6.8 mi · ~520m (1,706 ft) gain · Moderate · ~3.5–4 hrs round trip.

💡 For the relaxed group who want to see the lake without the full hike: the Miradouro da Lagoa do Fogo viewpoint is accessible by car and looks directly down into the crater. Completely stunning and zero elevation. Park at the top and enjoy the view while the hikers are on trail below.
Midday

Regroup and drive north (~25 min) to Ribeira Grande — the island's second city, charming, largely untouristy, and full of great lunch spots. Grab lunch here. While you're in town:

Walk the old historic center, the Ponte das Três Arcos (a lovely old stone bridge), and peek into the Igreja do Espírito Santo. Ribeira Grande has a completely different energy from Ponta Delgada — quieter, more local, worth 45 minutes of wandering.

💡 The Associação Agrícola (the legendary local steak spot) is right here in Ribeira Grande — great lunch option if you want to hit it while you're already here rather than making a special trip.
Afternoon

After lunch, the group splits by energy:

Adventurous group: Salto do Cabrito Waterfall trail (PRC29, departs near Caldeiras da Ribeira Grande hot springs). A varied trail through forest, past geothermal springs, along a river canyon, and up metal stairs to the base of a dramatic waterfall. ~5 miles, medium difficulty. Allow 3–3.5 hrs.

Relaxed group: Drive 15 min east along the north coast to Gorreana Tea Plantation — Europe's only tea plantation, family-owned since 1883. Walk the rolling green rows, visit the old factory, taste the teas for free. Flat, beautiful, and completely relaxed. Buy some to bring home.

💡 Both groups can reconvene at Gorreana afterward — hikers finish in the same general area, so it works as a natural meeting point before heading south for dinner.
Evening

Drive south (~40 min) back to the houses. First group dinner out of the trip — try A Tasca in Ponta Delgada (arrive before 6pm, no reservations) or book ahead at another restaurant. You've earned it.

Leave by 7:30am — check webcam first Split-group friendly Lagoa do Fogo · Ribeira Grande · Salto do Cabrito · Gorreana Book dinner or arrive at A Tasca by 6pm
Day 3 · Monday, June 1
Whale Watching + Chapel + Dinner Out
Most memorable day
8:15 AM

Walk or drive 5 min to the Vila Franca do Campo marina. Check in with Futurismo Azores Adventures. Split into your boats:

🚢 Catamaran — Paula, MB, Amy & Beth · Smooth ride, more deck space, great sightlines. Ideal for anyone who prefers a stable, comfortable experience.

🚤 Zodiac — Kathie, Rachel, Emma, Meg, Casey & Nellie · Fast, low to the water, and closer to the animals when they surface. The wilder, more exhilarating ride.

⚠️ Zodiac riders: eat a light breakfast (not a big one), wear layers, and expect to get splashed. Motion sickness meds recommended if you're prone — take them 30–60 min before boarding.
9:00 AM

Both boats depart. ~3 hours on the Atlantic. June is one of the best months of the year — excellent for sperm whales, common and bottlenose dolphins, and sometimes blue whales or fin whales. Futurismo uses land-based spotters with binoculars to locate animals before the boats head out, giving a very high success rate.

💡 The zodiac gets lower and closer when an animal surfaces — unforgettable. The catamaran gives a broader view and a better vantage point when dolphins bow-ride.
Noon

Return to marina ~noon. Regroup, dry off, and compare stories over lunch. Grab something casual in Vila Franca do Campo — the village square has good spots — or head back to the house for a relaxed lunch on the terrace.

Afternoon

Free afternoon — a well-earned rest. Pool at the house, read, nap, or take a gentle walk through Vila Franca do Campo village.

For anyone with energy: hike up to the Chapel of Nossa Senhora da Paz — a 10-minute walk from the village center up a beautiful tiled staircase lined with azulejo panels. Panoramic views over town and the Atlantic from the top. A genuinely special spot that almost no tourists find.

💡 The chapel staircase is one of the island's hidden gems. Not in most guidebooks. The view from the top is completely worth it.
Evening

Celebrate the day with a proper group dinner out in Ponta Delgada — the whale watching high deserves it. Options:

Alcides — legendary Azorean steak with pimenta salgada. A classic, festive choice for a big group.

A Tasca — lively Azorean classics, no reservations, arrive before 6pm or prepare to wait.

💡 Book Alcides ahead for 12. Or do A Tasca if you're willing to arrive early — it's a great fit for the celebratory energy of this day.

Day 4 · Tuesday, June 2
Furnas — Volcano Day
A full group day
Morning

Caldeiras das Furnas (free, open air): walk among active fumaroles, boiling mud pots, and sulphur steam vents at Lagoa das Furnas. Around noon, watch staff pull out the cozido pots that have been cooking underground in the volcanic soil since morning. This is unlike anything you've ever seen.

Miradouro do Pico do Ferro for a beautiful high view over the whole valley.

💡 The sulphur smell hits you as you arrive in Furnas. It fades after a few minutes. Lean into it.
Midday

Cozido das Furnas — pre-booked at Tony's Restaurante or Caldeiras & Vulcões. A meat and vegetable stew slow-cooked underground for 5+ hours by volcanic heat alone. One of the most unique meals on earth.

⚠️ Must be reserved in advance — restaurants prepare it to order and quantities are limited.
Afternoon

Hot springs — choose your vibe:

Terra Nostra Park (€16/person): world-famous botanical garden + a huge iron-orange thermal pool (38–42°C). Renovated in 2024. Incredible garden to explore before soaking.

Poça da Dona Beija: 5 pools at different temperatures, more casual, open until 10pm.

⚠️ Dark swimsuits only. Both pools have iron-rich water that stains permanently.
Evening

Optional detour: Bar da Caloura (30 min on the way home) for fresh seafood right on the water at sunset. Or head home for a relaxed house dinner.

Pre-book cozido — essential Dark swimsuits! Everyone together Terra Nostra €16/person
Day 5 · Wednesday, June 3
Sete Cidades — the Iconic West
The flagship day
Very Early

Leave by 7:30am. This is important — Vista do Rei parking fills by 9am, and fog can roll in by mid-morning and ruin everything. Check the webcam before leaving.

⚠️ Sete Cidades fog warning: if the webcam shows thick cloud, consider flipping this day with another. The lakes are invisible from above in heavy fog — there's nothing to see.

Miradouro Vista do Rei — the postcard view of the Azores. Twin blue and green volcanic crater lakes spread out below you. Walk the old Monte Palace hotel ruins for an even higher angle.

Boca do Inferno viewpoint — a short 1.2km walk from Lagoa do Canário parking. Panoramic views of both lakes plus the Atlantic beyond.

💡 Do Vista do Rei first (more accessible), then Boca do Inferno. About 10 min drive apart.

Drive down into the caldera to the tiny village of Sete Cidades. Lunch at one of the small local spots in the village. Then the group splits:

Adventurous group: Vista do Rei to Sete Cidades hike (7.5 mi, 1,459 ft elevation, ~5 hrs) or Serra Devassa loop (2–2.5 hrs, beautiful 360° views).

Relaxed group: Stroll or rent bikes around the flat lake edge. Walk the old aqueduct. Sit by the water.

Afternoon

Caldeira Velha hot springs on the drive home (~€10/person). A lush jungle waterfall with a geothermal pool underneath it. Completely different vibe from Furnas — smaller, wilder, incredibly atmospheric.

⚠️ Dark swimsuits only — the water is iron-rich and stains.
Evening

Return home. Sunset from the house, dinner in or nearby in Vila Franca do Campo.

Day 7 · Friday, June 5
Vila Franca Islet + Last Evening
Last full day — make it count
Morning

The islet is 5 minutes from our door — the group splits by energy level for two different ways to experience the same iconic landmark:

🚤 Zodiac / Boat Tour (relaxed group) — A guided historical zodiac tour around the "Princess Ring," a perfectly circular submerged volcanic crater 500m offshore. ~1 hour. Biologist commentary on the geology and marine life. Book through Terra Azul or Azores Whale Watch.

🛶 Kayak Tour (adventurous group) — Departs 9:30am from the marina. ~3 hours paddling through sea caves, circumnavigating the crater, and landing on the small beach inside the islet to explore on foot. Since the lagoon doesn't open for swimming until June 15, this is actually the *best* way to get inside. Operators: Epic Volcanic (wooden house at the end of the marina — look for the green kayaks) or Fun Activities Azores. Both use sit-on-top tandem kayaks, stable for beginners.

💡 Kayak riders: book ahead for a group and confirm they can handle your party size. The 25–30 min paddle to the islet is easy-moderate. Dry bags are provided for phones and valuables. Pick up a queijada at Queijadas do Morgado (a few doors down from the marina) before you launch.
⚠️ Kayaking is weather-dependent. If seas are rough on June 5, the boat tour is the reliable fallback — always worth it.
Afternoon

Regroup after the morning on the water. Head to Ponta Delgada — visit a pineapple plantation if you didn't on arrival day (Augusto Arruda, just outside the city). Last afternoon at the houses: swim, pack, unwind. Soak up the last of it.

Evening

Final dinner out — the last night together deserves something special. Book ahead in Ponta Delgada:

Tasquinha Vieira: the best restaurant on the island. Modern Azorean fusion, seasonal sharing plates, exceptional wine list. Reserve well in advance for 12.

Alcides: legendary Azorean steak with pimenta salgada. A fitting, festive last meal on the island.

💡 After dinner, take a slow walk along the Ponta Delgada waterfront promenade. Harbor lights, warm June air, last night in the Azores. 🌊

On foot
Hike guide

São Miguel has some of the best hiking in the North Atlantic. Here are the standouts — ranked roughly by how much we'd regret skipping them.

🏆 Lagoa do Fogo — The Unmissable One
6.8 mi · Moderate ~4 hrs · 1,706 ft gain

The best hike on the island that we don't have in the itinerary yet. Lagoa do Fogo ("Lake of Fire") is a turquoise volcanic crater lake deep in the island's interior — dramatic, almost otherworldly. The trail winds through lush jungle and water channels before the lagoon opens up in front of you. On a clear day it's arguably the most beautiful single view on São Miguel.

When: Best added to Sunday 5/31 (swap or combine with the Gorreana day — it's on the way back from the north coast) or as a morning activity on Tuesday 6/2 before Furnas. It's in the center of the island, close to home base. Leave early — parking fills and mist can roll in by 10am.

💡 The guided hiking tour through Fun Activities Azores (€53/person) includes transport and a naturalist guide — worth it if you want the full context of the volcano's geology.
🌊 Salto do Prego / Sanguinho Trail
2.8 mi · Easy 1.5–2 hrs

A lush loop through jungle-dense forest to three cascading waterfalls, including Cascata da Gruta (Cave Waterfall) — one of the most beautiful on the island. Swimming holes at the base of each fall. The trail also passes through Sanguinho, a hauntingly beautiful abandoned village. Easy enough for most of the group.

When: Perfect add-on after Furnas day (Tue 6/2) — it's about 30 min from Furnas, near Faial da Terra. Do the caldeiras and cozido in the morning, skip the hot springs that afternoon, and hike to the waterfalls instead for a swim. Or save it for the adventurous subgroup on any slower day.

💡 Bring swimwear — the waterfall pools are the whole point. Cobbled path after rain is slippery; trail shoes essential.
👁️ Janela do Inferno — Hidden Gem
Short · Easy-Moderate ~2 hrs

One of the most underrated short hikes on the island. You walk through jungle tunnels, over old aqueducts, along moss-covered walls, and arrive at a bizarre vertical rock face where water literally bursts out of a hole in the cliff — the "Window to Hell." Short, weird, and genuinely thrilling in a way the popular trails aren't. Bring a small flashlight for the tunnels.

When: Perfect pairing with the Nordeste day (Thu 6/4) — it's near Lagoa municipality, en route back from Nordeste. A 2-hour detour that feels like a secret. Best after recent rain when the spring is flowing strongest.

⚠️ Trail shoes with good grip are essential — wet stones throughout. Lightweight jacket recommended.
⛰️ Pico da Vara — Summit Climb
Challenging Permit required

The highest point on São Miguel at 1,105m. The trail climbs through the Tronqueira Forest, a protected conservation area and last refuge of the extremely rare Azores bullfinch (Priolo). Serious hikers only — this is a half-day commitment. The summit views on a clear day are 360° across the entire island.

When: Could replace the Nordeste drive on Thu 6/4 for the adventurous subgroup. Important: a permit is required and must be reserved well in advance through the Azores parks system. Research and book before you travel.

💡 Even if you don't summit, hiking into Tronqueira Forest on its own is exceptional — ancient laurisilva woodland, endemic birds, true wilderness.

Insider knowledge
Tips & tricks
🌫️

Check webcams every morning

São Miguel weather changes fast. Sete Cidades viewpoints can be completely fogged over. Check live webcam apps before driving 45 min to find nothing. We've built in flexibility for exactly this.

⏰

Early bird wins at viewpoints

Vista do Rei parking fills by 9–10am on clear days. Arrive before 9am for the best light, fewest people, and a parking spot. Worth the early alarm.

👙

Dark swimsuits for hot springs

Iron-rich water at Furnas's hot springs permanently stains light-colored swimwear orange. This is a serious warning. Pack dark or old swimsuits specifically for hot spring days.

🌧️

Always pack a rain layer

Even on beautiful days, mountain temperatures drop and showers appear out of nowhere. A packable rain jacket is essential. Layers always.

💶

Carry cash

Small villages, trail parking, and some local restaurants only accept cash. Get euros at an ATM in Ponta Delgada when you arrive.

🚗

Driving is easy

Roads are well-maintained, GPS works perfectly, traffic is light. Mountain roads can be narrow — take them slowly. No toll roads on São Miguel.

🍽️

Restaurants open late

Most restaurants don't open for dinner until 6:30–7pm. Don't show up at 6pm expecting to eat. Plan afternoon snacks if hunger strikes earlier.

💰

Tipping culture

Tipping is not obligatory in Portugal, but rounding up to the nearest 10% is always appreciated for good service. Never expected, always welcomed.

📱

Your US phone works

Portugal is EU — most US plans include international data. Confirm with your carrier before leaving. Google Maps works well across the island.

🔌
Power adapters — everyone needs one
Portugal uses Type F plugs — two round pins, 230V / 50Hz. US plugs will not fit. Pick up a Type F adapter or universal travel adapter before you leave. All modern phones, laptops, and camera chargers are dual-voltage (100–240V), so you only need the plug adapter — check your device's power brick. One adapter per person is ideal; a multi-port USB hub + one adapter works well for shared rooms.

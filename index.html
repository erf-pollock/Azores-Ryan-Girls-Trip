🏡 Our houses
🏠 Casa do Mar ↗🏠 Casa da Ilha ↗
São Miguel is known as the "Green Island" — a wild, living landscape of crater lakes, volcanic hot springs, dramatic cliffs, whale-filled Atlantic waters, and endless hydrangea-lined roads. It manages to be simultaneously adventurous and deeply relaxing. There is something here for every single person in this group.
Our houses are in Ponta Garça, on the south-central coast — a perfect home base. Ponta Delgada (the main city) is 20 min west. Furnas valley is 40 min east. The Vila Franca islet is 5 minutes from our door.

Insider knowledge
Tips & tricks
🌫️

Check webcams every morning

São Miguel weather changes fast. Check Sete Cidades and Lagoa do Fogo webcams before driving. Built-in flexibility is the key to a great trip.

⏰

Early bird wins at viewpoints

Vista do Rei parking fills by 9–10am on clear days. Arrive before 9am for the best light, fewest people, and a parking spot.

👙

Dark swimsuits for hot springs

Iron-rich water at Furnas and Caldeira Velha permanently stains light-colored swimwear orange. Pack dark or old swimsuits specifically for these days.

🌧️

Always pack a rain layer

Even on beautiful days, mountain temperatures drop and showers appear out of nowhere. A packable rain jacket is essential.

💶

Carry cash

Small villages, trail parking, and some local restaurants only accept cash. Get euros at an ATM in Ponta Delgada when you arrive.

🚗

Driving is easy

Roads are well-maintained, GPS works perfectly, traffic is light. Mountain roads can be narrow — take them slowly. No toll roads on São Miguel.

🍽️

Restaurants open late

Most restaurants don't open for dinner until 6:30–7pm. Plan afternoon snacks if hunger strikes earlier.

💰

Tipping culture

Tipping is not obligatory in Portugal, but rounding up ~10% is always appreciated. Never expected, always welcomed.

📱

Your US phone works

Portugal is EU — most US plans include international data. Confirm with your carrier before leaving.

🔌
Power adapters — everyone needs one
Portugal uses Type F plugs — two round pins, 230V / 50Hz. US plugs will not fit. Pick up a Type F or universal travel adapter before you leave. All modern phones, laptops, and camera chargers are dual-voltage (100–240V) — check your power brick. One adapter per person is ideal.
